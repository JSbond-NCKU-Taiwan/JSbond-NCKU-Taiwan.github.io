Overview

Idea

Human bodies are composed of millions of chemicals which regulate our physiological activities and homeostasis. Among these chemicals, one specific protein marks as the definitive factor due to its relations with inflammation, cancer, autoimmune disorders and numerous significant diseases. This protein is observed to be overexpressed in cytoplasm whenever these pathological changes occur, and it exerts its influence by stabilizing and controlling mRNA translation. This critical adaptor protein is called “Human antigen R (HuR)”.

As we learned the character of HuR in pathogenesis, we wondered the possibility to inhibit downstream signaling by interfering the binding between HuR and mRNA. To validate our idea, we designed a series of experiments targeting at a commonly seen, easily observed disease regulated by HuR, Psoriasis.

First, we substituted the specific HuR-binding mRNA fragment with peptides, which perform great potential in pharmaceutical application owing to its high compatibility, specificity, low cytotoxicity, and a wide range of modifications as functional structures. We screened through twenty amino acids and isolated one specialized type as modification target according to the result of Hot-Lig (a binding affinity prediction software). The ultimately created nanoparticle has higher permeability, stability and transportability, which overcame the major setbacks of previous peptide drugs. It was soon named” JS-1”, the leading character in our play!

Wanna dive deeper into the JS-1 adventure with us? We are proud to present our research journey in following articles. You can comprehend the properties of HuR in “HuR introduction” while "JS-1 Design” provides you with an abstract of how JS-1 was designed and examined. Don’t forget to check out” Experiment” for detailed design process and models elaboration, and a click on” Discussion” opens up a mind-blowing exploration of JS-1 possibilities with us!

[JS-1 performed well both in vitro and in vivo. We set up imiquimod-induced dermatitis in mice that closely resembles human psoriasis lesions, and intraperitoneally inject HuR inhibitor JS-1 evaluate its therapeutic effects (as the drawing below). It turned out that JS-1 was highly effective. As a series of following outcomes brought out more insights, we found that JS-1 could not only meet clinical needs but also serve as a prominent drug carrier itself! JS-1 draws a silver lining on forthcoming clinical applications and further exploration in the future.]
